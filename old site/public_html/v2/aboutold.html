About Us

The University of Toronto Seismic Design Team (UTSD) aims to promote seismic engineering to the undergraduate engineering student body. UTSD consists of both undergraduate and graduate students from both the engineering and architectural faculties collaborating to design a scale high rise structure out of balsa wood. The team will compete in the Annual Seismic Design Competition hosted by the Earthquake Engineering Research Institute (EERI) in which universities from around the world test their structures on a shake table competing in seismic performance, predictions, and cost-analysis. The Seismic Design Team aims to represent Skule at a competition level while providing students with the chance to be involved in a multi-disciplinary, complex design challenge.

Analysis Techniques

The analysis of structures to earthquake ground motion is a well understood problem with some well-developed underlying theory. Typically, all that is known in seismic design is the expected peak acceleration that may be experienced. However, with our design team, we work with the ground motion acceleration data, which provides a more study-friendly approach, and is available in certain situations. As a brief outline we will comment on the use of Frequency Analysis in determining the natural frequency of the structure. Then we will outline two general approaches we as a team use to determine the earthquake response of our structure; Time History Analysis and Response Spectrum Analysis. All of the methods described below can be implemented in SAP2000 which is our design team’s program of choice. Along the way we will provide some images from the program to help visualize the process.
